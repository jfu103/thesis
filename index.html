how to have human relationships.

intimacy is defined not only more than how warm and fuzzy someone makes us feel; often times it is fought out over how boundaries are drawn and violated. the closer someone is to you, the more likely or able they are to cause you bodily or emotional harm.

the following is an examination of how individual 04291997 (fu, kei) constructs their social relationships: first through an invitation where they draw boundaries, and then when those are inevitably violated, a citation that outlines the wrongs and attempts to inform the violator of what went wrong.

as primitive as such means are to establishing conductive conversations about how intimate relationships should work, the sterilization of language and form around intimate relationships perhaps suggests that individual 04291997 (aka, "the subject") only feels safe to discuss intimacy when the burden of emotional attachment is absent.
